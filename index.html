Suhyun Jeon

I am an Integrated M.S./Ph.D. student at the Graduate School of Convergence Science and Technology, Seoul National University, advised by Professor Jaeheung Park. My research lies at the intersection of robotic manipulation, motion planning, and machine learning, with a focus on learning representations and motion primitives for reliable robot-arm manipulation in constrained and uncertain environments.

I am particularly interested in connectivity-aware constrained motion planning, viscosity-adaptive pouring manipulation, bimanual manipulation, and failure detection for learning-based robot policies.

Research Interests

My research aims to develop robot systems that can plan, adapt, and recover robustly in complex manipulation tasks. I work on learning-based constrained motion planning, representation learning for robot configuration spaces, adaptive motion generation for non-rigid object and liquid manipulation, and bimanual manipulation with dual robot arms. I am also interested in integrating failure detection and recovery mechanisms into imitation-learning and motion-generation pipelines.

Projects
Adaptive Motion Manifold Primitives for Robotic Pouring
Robotic manipulation, motion primitive learning, conditional generative models
Designing a trajectory generation framework that adapts pouring motions to different liquid viscosities and container geometries, with real-robot execution on Franka Panda.

Connectivity-Aware Start-Goal Selection for Constrained Motion Planning
Motion planning, representation learning, contrastive learning
Learning a configuration-space representation that reflects connectivity under constraints, and using it to select feasible IK pairs before running sampling-based planners.

Dual-Arm Virtual Gripper for Large-Object Manipulation
Bimanual manipulation, grasp synthesis, dual-arm control
Investigating how two robot arms can act as a virtual gripper to grasp and manipulate objects that exceed the size limits of a single parallel gripper.

Failure Detection for Learning-Based Robot Policies
Imitation learning, uncertainty estimation, robot safety
Studying failure prediction and out-of-distribution detection for robot manipulation policies using multimodal observations and conformal thresholding.
