Suhyun Jeon

I am an Integrated M.S./Ph.D. student at the Graduate School of Convergence Science and Technology, Seoul National University, advised by Professor Jaeheung Park. My research focuses on robotic manipulation, constrained motion planning, and machine learning for reliable robot-arm systems. I am particularly interested in learning representations and motion-generation methods that improve planning, adaptation, and execution in high-dimensional and constrained robotic tasks.

Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of robotic manipulation, motion planning, and machine learning. I work on learning-based constrained motion planning, connectivity-aware representations of robot configuration spaces, adaptive motion generation for non-rigid and liquid manipulation, learning-based sampling strategies, bimanual manipulation, and haptic telemanipulation systems.

Projects
Bimanual Manipulation and Virtual Gripper Control
Dual-arm manipulation, grasp synthesis, robot-arm coordination
Investigating dual-arm manipulation methods where two robot arms can act as a virtual gripper for grasping and manipulating objects that exceed the size limits of a single robot gripper. The project explores Dex-Net-style grasp mapping, coordination constraints, sliding-chain constraints, and execution strategies for bimanual manipulation.
